Cat Travel Requirements from the United States to Mexico

1. Rabies Vaccination

Your cat must have been vaccinated against rabies at least 15 days prior to entering Mexico. The rabies vaccination certificate must include:

  • Cat's identification (name, breed, age)
  • Rabies vaccine manufacturer
  • Vaccination date
  • Vaccine expiration date
  • Veterinarian’s signature

2. Health Certificate

A health certificate is no longer required for cats traveling from the USA to Mexico. However, your cat must be free from contagious diseases and parasites.

3. External and Internal Parasites Treatment

  • Cats should be treated for external parasites (fleas and ticks) and internal parasites (worms) before traveling to Mexico.
  • Signs of parasites during the inspection in Mexico may result in treatment, at the owner’s expense, by Mexican authorities (SENASICA).

4. Inspection Upon Arrival

Upon entry into Mexico, your cat will be inspected by the Mexican Agricultural Health Authority (SENASICA) to ensure it is free of diseases and parasites.

Source: APHIS - Pet Travel from the USA to Mexico
