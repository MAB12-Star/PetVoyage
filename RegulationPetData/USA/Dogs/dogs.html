Requirements for Bringing Dogs into the USA from Mexico and Canada

Rabies Vaccination and Microchip Requirements

All dogs imported into the United States, including those from Mexico and Canada, must meet the rabies vaccination and microchip requirements set by the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC). Below are the key points based on the country of origin:

1. Dogs from Mexico

Mexico is classified as a low-risk country for rabies. The following requirements apply for dogs entering the United States from Mexico:

  • All dogs must have a valid rabies vaccination certificate.
  • The rabies vaccination certificate must include the following details:
    • Dog’s identification and age
    • Rabies vaccine manufacturer
    • Vaccination date
    • Vaccine expiration date
    • Veterinarian’s signature
  • All dogs must be microchipped. The microchip must be ISO-compliant and readable at the port of entry.
  • Importers may need to submit an Import Permit from the CDC, which must be approved before arrival in the USA, depending on specific cases.

For more detailed information, visit the official CDC page: CDC - Rabies-Free and Low-Risk Countries.

2. Dogs from Canada

Canada is also classified as a rabies-free country, but some regulations still apply:

  • Dogs must be vaccinated against rabies and have a valid rabies vaccination certificate.
  • All dogs must be microchipped. The microchip must be ISO-compliant and readable at the port of entry.
  • Dogs must be in good health and may be subject to inspection upon arrival at the U.S. border.

Additional APHIS Requirements

In addition to CDC rabies and microchip requirements, the Animal and Plant Health Inspection Service (APHIS) may have specific regulations for dogs entering the United States from other countries, including Mexico and Canada.

1. Health Certificate

Dogs imported into the U.S. may require a health certificate issued by a veterinarian. This certificate must confirm that the dog is free from any infectious diseases, such as screwworm and foot-and-mouth disease.

2. Quarantine

If a dog shows signs of illness or comes from a country affected by certain diseases (such as screwworm or foot-and-mouth disease), the dog may be subject to quarantine until it is deemed healthy for entry.

3. Screwworm and Foot-and-Mouth Disease

  • Dogs coming from regions affected by screwworm must have a certificate issued by a veterinary official stating that the dog has been inspected and is free from screwworm.
  • Dogs from foot-and-mouth disease regions must be bathed, and bedding must be free from dirt, hay, or straw.

For more information on APHIS regulations for bringing dogs from other countries into the USA, visit: APHIS - Bringing Dogs into the USA.

Documents and Forms Required

  • CDC Importation Form - This form must be submitted and approved before the arrival of any dog from a low-risk country like Mexico.
  • APHIS Health Certificate Requirements - This page provides detailed health certificate requirements for dogs from other countries.
